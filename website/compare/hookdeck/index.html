Fit-based comparison

Hookwing vs Hookdeck

If you want the short version, Hookwing is the better fit for teams that want agent-ready webhook infrastructure, lower starting cost, and a simpler path from API key to live endpoint. Hookdeck is the better fit if you need a mature inbound event gateway with metered scale controls, issues tooling, and metrics export today.

Start with Hookwing See the breakdown
No chest-beating. No fake parity. Just the tradeoffs that matter.
Best fit for Hookwing API-first and agent-friendly teams

Hookwing starts at $19/month, has a larger free tier than Hookdeck, and gives you native API signup plus MCP support out of the box.

Best fit for Hookdeck Event gateway-heavy setups

Hookdeck's pricing page currently emphasizes throughput controls, metrics export, issues management, and SSO on higher tiers.

Shared baseline Both cover core webhook ops

Automatic retries, webhook signing, replay, and event inspection are table stakes on both sides.

Decision shortcut

Choose based on operating model, not logo preference

The cleanest split is this: Hookwing is trying to make webhook infrastructure easier to operate for software teams and autonomous systems. Hookdeck is trying to give event-heavy teams more gateway control and observability depth.

Choose Hookwing if you want

A lighter operational model, lower starting price, and infrastructure that already fits API-driven and agent-driven workflows.

  • $19/month entry tier instead of $39/month.
  • 25,000 free events per month instead of 10,000.
  • 7-day free retention instead of 3 days.
  • Native API signup and endpoint provisioning flows.
  • MCP support without routing through a separate CLI layer.

Choose Hookdeck if you want

A more explicitly event-gateway-oriented platform with pricing that leans into throughput, issue handling, and exported metrics.

  • Metered event pricing and additional throughput controls.
  • Built-in issues management on the pricing surface.
  • Metrics export called out on the Growth tier.
  • SSO, SAML, and SCIM already listed on paid plans.
  • A product centered around connections, transformations, and event processing flow control.

Pricing shape

The pricing gap is real, and it changes who each product serves well

This is the clearest reason someone evaluates Hookwing against Hookdeck in the first place. One product starts cheaper and stays simpler. The other starts higher and leans harder into operational controls.

Hookwing Paper Plane $0

25,000 events/month, 7-day retention, API access, and MCP support. Good for a real trial, not just a toy sandbox.

Hookwing Warbird / Stealth Jet $19 → $89

This is Hookwing's strongest fit window: small teams, growing SaaS products, and agent-first builders that want real capacity before entering enterprise pricing territory.

Hookdeck Team / Growth $39 → $499

Hookdeck gives a clearer event-gateway and observability story at the higher end, but the jump is much steeper for teams that just need solid webhook infrastructure.
